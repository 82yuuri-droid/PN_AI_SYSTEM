# CEO Business Review: Path to 10,000,000 Yen Monthly Profit

## Executive Diagnosis

PN does not have a creativity problem, an AI problem, or a documentation problem.

The single biggest constraint is that the business has not yet converted its capability into a focused high-ticket sales engine.

The repo shows strong philosophy, useful service areas, practical automation thinking, and early operating data. But it also shows a dangerous pattern: many possible services, many possible automations, many possible templates, many possible AI agents, and not enough evidence of a narrow, repeatable path from qualified lead to high-ticket close to profitable delivery.

Brutally honest conclusion:

PN will not reach 10,000,000 yen monthly profit by becoming a better SNS/video production shop. It can reach that level by becoming a producer-led business growth and AI operating system company that sells high-ticket outcomes, then uses SNS, video, recruiting, and AI as delivery modules.

## 1. Single Biggest Bottleneck

The biggest bottleneck preventing 10,000,000 yen monthly profit is:

**No concentrated, repeatable high-ticket sales system.**

Current evidence:

- Sample monthly profit is 760,000 yen.
- Current sample projects are 250,000-500,000 yen each.
- The roadmap correctly identifies that 10,000,000 yen profit requires a 13x jump from the sample baseline.
- The business has many service categories, but only the latest roadmap sharply limits the offers.
- Existing docs emphasize automation and operations, but the largest missing business asset is a live sales pipeline with qualified buyers, proposal value, close probability, and next actions.

What this means:

- More templates will not solve the problem.
- More AI agents will not solve the problem.
- More content will not solve the problem.
- Better delivery alone will not solve the problem.

The bottleneck is getting enough high-quality sales conversations with buyers who have money, urgency, authority, and a clear economic problem.

## 2. Highest ROI Opportunity

The highest ROI opportunity is:

**Productize and sell AI Business System Builds to existing revenue-generating businesses.**

Why this is highest ROI:

- It matches the repo's strongest operational insight: clients need systems that connect sales, content, delivery, reporting, and improvement.
- It has higher pricing power than SNS posting or video production.
- It helps others make money or save labor, making ROI easier to justify.
- PN can use its own operating system as a demo and proof asset.
- It creates reusable assets: diagnostic, workflow maps, reports, dashboards, SOPs, prompts, templates, and case studies.
- It can convert into ongoing retainers.

Ideal offer:

- Paid diagnostic: 300,000 yen
- AI Business System Build: 2,000,000-5,000,000 yen
- Ongoing improvement retainer: 500,000-1,500,000 yen/month

Best buyers:

- Businesses already doing at least 10,000,000 yen monthly revenue
- Agencies and service businesses with manual delivery/reporting pain
- Recruiting-heavy companies with content and candidate-flow problems
- Clinics, schools, gyms, local chains, education businesses, and creator-led businesses with repeatable operations
- Companies where faster follow-up, better reporting, content systems, or AI workflows can create measurable money

## 3. The 20% Producing 80% of Results

The 20% of activities most likely to produce 80% of results:

1. Direct sales to warm, referred, and high-fit buyers
2. High-ticket proposals tied to money outcomes
3. Paid AI diagnostics that convert into implementation builds
4. Existing-client upsells into video, recruiting, campaign, or AI systems
5. Case studies and proof assets from every project
6. Simple delivery SOPs that protect margin
7. Weekly pipeline review focused on booked profit and next action

Everything else is secondary until the revenue engine is working.

## 4. Stop Immediately

Stop or pause these activities immediately:

- Selling custom work below 500,000 yen unless it is a strategic entry into a larger account
- Unpaid diagnostics
- Broad service menus that make PN look like a general SNS/video vendor
- Building AI agents before there is a paid workflow to support
- Creating templates that are not used in an active sale or delivery
- Publishing content without a direct sales or proof function
- Accepting projects without a clear money outcome
- Taking meetings with no decision-maker, budget signal, or urgent problem
- Full-time hiring before predictable revenue
- Over-optimizing internal systems while the sales pipeline is thin
- Treating SNS operation, video production, recruiting support, and AI support as separate businesses

The harsh truth:

If an activity does not create sales conversations, increase proposal value, improve close rate, protect margin, or become a proof asset, it is probably a distraction right now.

## 5. Ideal Business Model for PN

PN should become:

**A producer-led business growth and AI operating system studio.**

Not:

- SNS agency
- Video production company
- Recruiting support vendor
- AI tool installer
- Generic marketing consultant

Core positioning:

PN helps valuable people, products, and culture reach the people who need them by building the strategy, content, campaigns, and AI operating systems that turn attention into money, hiring, trust, and durable assets.

## Business Model Rules

- The business sells outcomes, not deliverables.
- SNS, video, recruiting, and AI are modules inside higher-value offers.
- Every client project must produce at least one reusable asset.
- Every diagnostic must lead to a yes/no implementation proposal.
- Every retainer must have a monthly money outcome or strategic asset outcome.
- Every project under 500,000 yen needs a clear strategic reason.
- The sales pipeline is the operating center of the company.

## Brutal CEO Notes

1. PN has enough ideas. It needs fewer offers and more selling.
2. The current 250,000-500,000 yen project size is structurally too small for the profit ambition.
3. AI is not the business unless it is sold as a business outcome.
4. SNS and video are not the center. They are delivery modules inside a higher-value growth system.
5. The most important document missing is not another strategy memo. It is a real pipeline with names, deal values, next actions, and close dates.
6. The founder should spend the best energy on sales, premium positioning, strategic relationships, and quality control.
7. If PN cannot say how a project helps the client make money, save time, hire better, or build durable assets, the project should not be sold.
