# 90-Day Roadmap: Ruthless Path to 10 Million Yen Monthly Profit

## Strategic Goal

Reach 10,000,000 yen monthly profit without building a fragile service business.

The mission remains: help valuable people, products, and culture reach the people who need them. The constraint is profit. For the next 90 days, anything that does not create revenue, protect margin, or become a reusable asset is secondary.

## The Hard Truth

The previous roadmap had the right direction but too many parallel priorities. It assumed PN could simultaneously:

- Sell five high-ticket retainers
- Sell many diagnostics
- Build multiple AI agents
- Create case studies
- Hire multiple roles
- Package several offers
- Publish owned media
- Build dashboards and operating systems

That is too much surface area for a 90-day profit sprint.

The fastest path to 10,000,000 yen monthly profit is not broad system-building. It is closing a small number of high-ticket deals with urgent economic value, using simple assets and AI only where they increase sales speed or delivery margin.

## Challenged Assumptions

### 1. Five 1,500,000 yen retainers may be slower than two or three larger outcome-based projects

Retainers are attractive because they recur, but they often require trust, proof, and a longer sales cycle. A faster path may be:

- 2 strategic build projects at 3,000,000-5,000,000 yen each
- 2 retainers at 1,500,000 yen/month
- 3 paid diagnostics that convert into builds

Retainers should remain a target, but large outcome-based projects may get to profit faster.

### 2. Paid diagnostics are useful only if they are a sales mechanism, not a separate business line

Selling 20 diagnostics is a distraction if they do not convert. The diagnostic should exist to create implementation projects. A diagnostic that does not lead to a build, retainer, or campaign is low leverage.

### 3. AI agents should not be built before there is repeated workflow pressure

Building six agents before the sales engine is proven risks creating internal tools for a business that has not yet closed enough high-value demand. In the next 90 days, AI should support only:

- Proposal speed
- Diagnostic delivery
- Monthly reporting and upsells
- Project risk detection

Everything else waits.

### 4. Hiring too early can reduce focus

Hiring five roles before revenue is locked creates management overhead. The first hires should only protect sales and delivery:

- Sales / partnership operator
- Producer assistant / project manager
- Specialist bench, not full hires

### 5. Public content is not the main bottleneck

Owned media matters long term, but 10,000,000 yen monthly profit in 90 days will most likely come from warm relationships, referrals, direct outreach, and high-trust proposals. Public content should support credibility, not consume prime selling hours.

## Activities to Eliminate for 90 Days

Stop or postpone:

- Low-ticket public products
- Broad owned media programs
- Complex dashboards that do not trigger sales actions
- AI agents not tied to proposals, diagnostics, reporting, or risk
- Custom one-off work below 500,000 yen unless it leads to a larger deal
- Unpaid diagnostics
- Strategy work without implementation path
- Meetings with no budget, decision-maker, or specific project
- Building templates that are not used in an active sale or delivery
- Hiring full-time roles before predictable revenue

## The Only 3 Offers for the Next 90 Days

### Offer 1: AI Business System Build

Primary offer.

Positioning:

Build AI-assisted business systems that increase revenue, reduce labor, improve reporting, and make operations repeatable.

Best buyers:

- Companies with existing revenue and operational pain
- Agencies, clinics, schools, recruiting companies, creators, ecommerce brands, local service businesses
- Teams with manual reporting, sales follow-up, content production, recruiting workflows, or client delivery bottlenecks

Price:

- Diagnostic: 300,000 yen
- Build: 2,000,000-5,000,000 yen
- Ongoing improvement retainer: 500,000-1,500,000 yen/month

Required proof:

- Before/after workflow map
- Estimated hours saved
- Revenue or margin impact
- Deliverables list
- 30-day implementation roadmap

### Offer 2: Producer Retainer

Secondary offer.

Positioning:

Act as the producer who turns valuable people, products, or culture into reach, trust, campaigns, and sales opportunities.

Best buyers:

- Founders with strong products but weak distribution
- Experts or creators with under-monetized attention
- Cultural projects that need positioning, campaign direction, and partnerships
- Companies preparing a launch

Price:

- 1,000,000-1,500,000 yen/month
- 3-month minimum

Required proof:

- Producer operating scope
- Monthly KPI plan
- Distribution map
- Campaign calendar
- Case examples or teardown examples

### Offer 3: Launch / Revenue Campaign

Selective offer.

Positioning:

Produce a focused campaign that turns a product, recruitment need, or cultural project into measurable reach, leads, applications, or sales.

Best buyers:

- Existing clients
- Warm referrals
- Products with a launch date
- Recruiting campaigns with urgent hiring needs

Price:

- 1,500,000-3,000,000 yen

Required proof:

- Campaign thesis
- Audience map
- Content and distribution plan
- Conversion path
- Reporting cadence

## Sales Operating Rules

For 90 days, sales activity outranks internal building.

Daily minimum:

- 5 warm relationship touches
- 5 targeted new outreaches
- 2 referral asks
- 1 proposal, diagnostic, or follow-up asset improved

Weekly minimum:

- 25 warm touches
- 25 targeted outreaches
- 10 referral asks
- 5 sales conversations
- 3 proposals sent
- 1 close or clear disqualification

Qualification rules:

- No decision-maker, no proposal.
- No urgent business problem, no custom strategy.
- No budget signal, sell diagnostic first.
- No implementation path, do not sell diagnostic.
- No clear client money outcome, do not accept project.

## Weekly Operating Cadence

### Monday: Money and Close Plan

- Update booked revenue, booked profit, collected cash, and profit gap
- Identify top 10 opportunities by expected profit and close probability
- Set weekly close targets
- Decide which proposals must go out this week

### Tuesday: Outreach and Referral Day

- Contact warm prospects
- Ask for referrals
- Follow up on open proposals
- Book sales calls
- Remove weak opportunities from the pipeline

### Wednesday: Sales Calls and Proposals

- Hold discovery calls
- Send proposals within 24 hours
- Convert diagnostics into build proposals
- Review pricing and scope for margin risk

### Thursday: Delivery and Margin Protection

- Review every active project
- Check scope, waiting tasks, revision risk, and client decision delays
- Create change orders where scope has expanded
- Extract one reusable asset from active delivery

### Friday: KPI Review and Proof

- Review weekly scorecard
- Publish or send one proof asset
- Update case study pack
- Decide next week's top 3 closes
- Stop any activity that did not move revenue, margin, or assets
