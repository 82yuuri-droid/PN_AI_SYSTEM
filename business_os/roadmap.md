# Roadmap

## Phase 1: Define the Producer Platform

### Goals

- Clarify producer identity
- Define the first target audience
- Create a simple public presence
- Build a repeatable way to explain the work

### Actions

- Write a one-page personal positioning statement
- Choose 2-3 focus areas: people, products, culture, or a specific niche inside them
- Create a portfolio page or simple website
- Publish 3-5 essays, case notes, or breakdowns showing taste and thinking
- Build a list of 25 people, products, or cultural projects worth helping

### Output

A clear public identity: what I do, who I help, and why it matters.

## Phase 2: Build Proof Through Small Projects

### Goals

- Create visible evidence of producer ability
- Learn what clients need most
- Refine offers through real work

### Actions

- Offer 3 pilot projects to high-potential people or products
- Document before, during, and after states
- Create case studies around positioning, launch, content, or distribution
- Ask for testimonials and referrals
- Track outcomes: reach, engagement, opportunities, sales, partnerships

### Output

Three strong proof points that show concrete value.

## Phase 3: Package the Business

### Goals

- Turn custom work into clear offers
- Create pricing and delivery structure
- Improve client acquisition

### Actions

- Package 2-3 core offers
- Define pricing ranges and timelines
- Create proposal templates
- Build an outreach list of aligned founders, creators, brands, and cultural operators
- Start a monthly newsletter or memo about valuable work that deserves more attention

### Output

A simple business engine: offer, audience, proof, outreach, delivery.

## Phase 4: Expand Reach and Partnerships

### Goals

- Move from service provider to trusted producer
- Build a network of collaborators
- Increase the scale of projects

### Actions

- Partner with designers, videographers, editors, strategists, and operators
- Create a repeatable launch playbook
- Host small salons, dinners, interviews, or digital events
- Build relationships with media, creators, communities, and distribution partners
- Select one flagship project to produce end to end

### Output

A growing producer network and larger cultural surface area.

## Phase 5: Build the Producer Studio

### Goals

- Develop a recognizable body of work
- Create assets beyond client services
- Build long-term leverage

### Actions

- Formalize the studio name, principles, and portfolio
- Create owned media: newsletter, podcast, video series, or publication
- Develop revenue share or equity-based partnerships
- Produce original cultural or product projects
- Build a small trusted team or bench of collaborators

### Output

A producer-led studio known for helping valuable things reach the people they are meant for.
