# A-LIGHT SNS Strategy

## Strategic Diagnosis

A-LIGHT's SNS should function as a recruiting trust engine.

The goal is not only to make A-LIGHT known. The goal is to make the right candidates feel:

- "I understand what A-LIGHT does."
- "I can imagine the people and culture."
- "I know what kind of person fits."
- "I feel safe enough to apply or ask questions."

## Business Goal

Increase talent applications and brand awareness.

## Primary Audience

Potential talent applicants who are evaluating whether A-LIGHT is a place where they can grow, belong, and build a meaningful career or creative path.

## Audience Questions

- What kind of company is A-LIGHT?
- What kind of people work there?
- What roles or opportunities exist?
- Will I fit the culture?
- What does growth look like?
- What happens after I apply?
- Is this credible and safe?

## Core Message

A-LIGHT is a place where talent can be discovered, developed, supported, and connected to real opportunities.

## Belief Shift Needed

Before:

- "I have heard the name, but I do not really understand what working with A-LIGHT is like."

After:

- "I understand the people, culture, expectations, and opportunity. I can imagine applying."

## Platform Roles

| Platform | Role |
| --- | --- |
| Instagram | Visual trust, people stories, culture, application reminders |
| TikTok / Reels | Discovery, short talent stories, day-in-the-life, role explanation |
| X / Threads | Thought leadership, hiring philosophy, quick FAQ, culture language |
| YouTube Shorts | Deeper short-form recruiting stories and recurring series |
| Website / Link Hub | Application conversion and candidate information |

## Content Pillars

## Pillar 1: People and Stories

Purpose:

- Make A-LIGHT feel human and credible.

Examples:

- Employee/talent interviews
- Day-in-the-life stories
- Why people joined
- Growth stories
- Team support moments

## Pillar 2: Work and Opportunity

Purpose:

- Help candidates understand roles, expectations, and possible futures.

Examples:

- Role explanations
- What a project looks like
- Skills that matter
- How people grow
- Career path examples

## Pillar 3: Culture and Values

Purpose:

- Make the brand memorable and help candidates self-select.

Examples:

- A-LIGHT's values
- How the team supports talent
- Behind-the-scenes culture
- Manager philosophy
- Standards and mindset

## Pillar 4: Application Confidence

Purpose:

- Remove hesitation before applying.

Examples:

- How to apply
- Interview process
- Common questions
- What to prepare
- What happens after application

## Pillar 5: Proof and Momentum

Purpose:

- Show that A-LIGHT creates real opportunities.

Examples:

- Results and milestones
- Event/activity recaps
- Talent achievements
- Media or project highlights
- Before/after growth stories

## Monthly Theme Plan

| Week | Theme | Goal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Week 1 | Who is A-LIGHT? | Awareness and brand clarity |
| Week 2 | People inside A-LIGHT | Trust and emotional connection |
| Week 3 | Opportunity and growth | Candidate motivation |
| Week 4 | How to apply / candidate FAQ | Conversion |

## Conversion Path

1. Candidate sees people/story content.
2. Candidate visits profile.
3. Candidate sees pinned "Start here" recruiting posts.
4. Candidate clicks application link or sends DM.
5. Candidate receives clear response and next step.
6. Candidate applies or books conversation.

## Recommended Profile Structure

- Bio: clear candidate-facing positioning
- Link: application or recruiting landing page
- Pinned post 1: What is A-LIGHT?
- Pinned post 2: Who fits A-LIGHT?
- Pinned post 3: How to apply
- Highlights:
  - People
  - Roles
  - FAQ
  - Culture
  - Apply

## Content Cadence

Minimum:

- 3 feed posts per week
- 2 short videos per week
- 3-5 stories per week
- 1 direct application CTA per week

Ideal:

- 5 feed/short-form posts per week
- 1 recurring interview series
- 1 candidate FAQ series
- 1 monthly application push

## Reusable Asset Opportunities

- Candidate FAQ library
- Employee/talent story bank
- Recruiting video templates
- Application process guide
- Interview preparation content
- Role explanation content
- Monthly recruiting report template

## Risks

- Content becomes generic employer branding
- Too much focus on awareness without application CTA
- Candidate journey after application is slow or unclear
- Lack of employee/talent story supply
- No KPI attribution from SNS to applications

## Next Action

Create a 30-day recruiting content calendar using the five content pillars and install an application tracking dashboard.
